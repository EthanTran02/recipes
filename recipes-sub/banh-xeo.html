Bánh Xèo: The Vietnamese Crispy Crepe Explained

Bánh Xèo is a savory Vietnamese crepe made with rice flour and coconut milk, resulting in a crispy, golden shell filled with pork, shrimp, and bean sprouts. Served with fresh lettuce leaves and a tangy dipping sauce, it's a delightful combination of flavors and textures

ingredients

  • Rice Flour & Turmeric
  • Coconut Milk
  • Pork & Shrimp
  • Bean Sprouts
  • Fresh Herbs & Vegetables

Steps

  • Prepare the Batter: Whisk together rice flour, turmeric, coconut milk, water, and salt. Let the batter rest for at least 30 minutes.
  • Cook the Filling: Sauté pork and shrimp until cooked through. Briefly stir-fry bean sprouts.
  • Make the Crepes: Heat a lightly oiled pan. Pour in a thin layer of batter and swirl to coat. Add pork, shrimp, and bean sprouts. Cover and cook until crispy. Fold in half.
  • Serve & Enjoy: Serve the Bánh Xèo hot with lettuce leaves, fresh herbs, and dipping sauce. Wrap a piece in lettuce, dip, and savor!
